Day 25 - Uroa, Zanzibar

Monday June 18th, 2001

Wake up at about 9 in the morning, go down change some money, have coffee in a bar, instant coffee, run into Christian. That's how the day all started. Bumping into Christian, this rastafarian streetboy in Dar.

Immediately he was trying to sell mea deal with the boat to zanziba. the deal was: I give hime the nmoney, he goes up, knows the people gives them the money, and then comes back to you with the tickets, at the residnets price, though. It seemed like n unappealing thing to do just to start with. First of all, why should I try to rip off this coutry that is trying to make a living out of tourism , by helpig a bum, second of all, the math didn't even add up! He was claiming he could get the tickets for 1500Tsh, but I had just checked, and the local rates were 8500,so both ways would be 17000 and he would be asking me for less money thanwhat he actuallyu needed, In addition, he was asking me to do what? Gve him the money, and wait for him to come back with the tickets? Ueah right. So instead I asked him for grass. Of course he could help me for that , ad hooked me up with two spliffs. Then he took me to smoke by the water, after having stopped and asked me to wait that he had to go pick something up Asked me to to sit ect to hime, gave me two spliffs as a gift, Itook them, so not to leavin the open and was trying to think on how that was not a good move, when a cop cameand told me that he saw everything and to go in the car.there they told me that Ihad to go to jailfor a day and taht the fine would ha ve been 3---USD. then he started asking for money. and that's when he statrted and he was yelling for more money threating meandI had to wip out thestash, since I did not have/ was not prepared to have to wip out more than 20 usd out. or 50 or so. and hekept onasking for more , and more, and when I got to a privateplace and counted how much i HAD LOST I HAD GAVE HIM, IT WAS 890USD. NOT BAD, HE, FOR A RASTAFARIAN.? THEY SAID HE WAS GOING TO HAVE TO GO TO JAIL, BUT OF COURESE IT WAS A NICE, CLEAR ObVIOUS SETUP.

And this anectdote, of course, made me think a lot about how America has spoiled me. In that it stopped my already started eduaction on how to cope with the fact of not being able to trust or rather treat everybody equally. But at that time , I was not happy to have to do it, and was trying to find a way out, But now that I realize, that there realy does not seem to be a way out, I am trying to be happy about the whole thing. I am still trying to decide whether to even just look at people that approach you, or not even that

This happened all just around noon, when I was supposed to take the ferry for Zanzibar at 1pm. When I got back to the hotel, to get my stuff, it was 20 to one and I left a message to the girs I had met the day bofore leaving my email address. Apparently, they were out to make the phone call. the guy that took us o the hotel the night bofore was also telling me that he saw the girls walking on the street just about half an hourh earlier. When I ran into him on the streets. So he probably was not llying. At any rate, I took a cab to the ferry, then hopped on, and headed to Zanzibar.

The ride was wet, as it started to rain. I met some french people, 2 guys, but they were being french and not too social. Then I met 2 norvegians at the immigration/tourist office. They were looking for a bus to go on the east coas of the island. Iwanted tog et out of the city after all tha had happened to me. I just felt like relaxing for a while, so I went with them, to this place were they were renting bungalows just on the beach. I got a single, and they got a room too.

When I got in the bungalow, that's where I counted the damage, how much I had given to the cop in the car. Then I went for a bath, but the low tide was making it extremeley annoying and shallow, so I soon came out and headed for dinner.

Adam is a nice guy, he works here. He tells me I can go back to the city tomorrow with him on the dala-dala, which he explains to me, means 5shilling-5shilling, sort of like nickel-nickel, because the bus used to cost one nickel once, instead of 200Tsh, and so they called it nickel-nickel to distinguish it from the plain ol' nickel.

Dinner was fun too, as we had it all together, and we got a little drunk and just kept on drinking and talking about norway, and politics, and WTO and IMF, and I think I want to go to Norway for a while.

I hung out on the beach, had a little to smoke, then went to bed.
