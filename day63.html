Day 63 - Addis Ababa, Ethiopia

Thursday July 26th, 2001

otcho (coccio): hard crust injera, brown.

Annababoro: double injera. Had it in debre damo.

I stopped at L.A. Burger on my way back from Memo Club, where I went to pick up the pocket knife that I forgot, which btw, I could not retrieve, as the guy with the key was not in, and I met this guy Girma Bizuayehu (09)227345. He offered to meet tomorrow @ 11am and drive down to Nazret with Yohannes, owner of pub and friend of Girma. And so I am thinking about it and I learn more Amharic as it keeps on pouring outside. I get home soaked.

charrask: i am finished; chaarasku: you are finished?

By the time I get to the Yemenia ticket office, it was like 5 or 6pm. I had to pick my visa extension too. I get in there, and they tell me that they tried to call me and were asking were the hell I was, that there was an opening at 1-2pm, and they needed my confirmation. I didn't even try to tell them that it would have been pretty stupid of me to stay at the hotel waiting for their phone call all day, and that I had other errands to run, and that it was pretty obvious that I wanted to be on that flight, since I was on the waiting list. So I was totally disappointed. I asked them to see if they could do something, and as they were typing hopelessly on those keyboard of their, I remember Douglas Adams and his hitchhikers tips. So I asked them for a phone and I made a last minute phone call to the Sudan Embassy, since I hadn't called them in a few days. And the guy said that yes, we can get the visa's. That we should bring the passports in tomorrow or Monday.

Wow, now that was some news. Sure I didn't even know how such a news would even sound. Well, now I do. And I head straight home with some happy news in my pocket.

I run into nairobi-peace-corp-buddies as I walk into my room, and I feel happy for the visa, but at the same time I feel sad, because Dan is not with me and he was supposed to come to Sudan with me. But the buddies, nairobi-peace-corp-buddies, tell me that "my friend is back". So I'm like "Wathefk? He left the-day-before-yesterday. Isn't due back for at least 10 days!". I go check with the hotel people and they tell me he is at Baro doing email. I run over there, in the rain, and see him, big-ol' Dan in that small room chatting with the owners of the Baro. What a nice vision. He had come back. He had thought about it again, and now wants to come back to go to Sudan with me.

Seeing Dan and hearing his 2 day story was just great. I loved it. And especially I loved his mosquito spray sound. Dan is great at sounds.

"Astera Aworka" good funk

He me up with his buddies and found out about this "Teddy Afro" gig tonite. Apparetly it's Teodros's band. Good jazz. They play live @ Coffee House in Sidistkilo. They played standards. The drummer lacks initative but is a good pocket. Very tight. Guitar absolutely amazing.

"Nazret Pension" Close to train station: attractive crazy girls. Younger than 20. All locals only 5birr.
