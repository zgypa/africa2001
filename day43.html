Day 43 - Addis Ababa, Ethiopia

Friday July 6th, 2001

Another crazy day. This time, when I woke up, at around 9, I didn't know if Afework was going to show up or not. But I did know one thing: I was not going to do much today. Only go to the Sudanese Embassy and drop off the VISA application. And then come back to bed and rest.

Afework showed up around 10.this was very good. So I told him the situation, and off we went. We took the taxi/bus that was yelling Kera-Mexico on Churchill Rd. to go to the embassy. We got to the embassy, dropped off the application. The funny thing was that we ran into the same guy we ran into yesterday, in the same exact spot, as we was walking out of the room in the same way he was yesterday. He asked me if I had the letter. I said yes, then he asked Afework if he had any business to take care of. To which he answered that he was with me. The guy said then (in Amharic) "Then your place is outside!" that just to indicate what kind of a person he was. In the office I filled out the application, dropped off two pictures, and he said to come back on Tuesday. No fee. On Tuesday I would be told if I would be granted the VISA or not. Only then I could actually start the application process.

After the embassy business was taken care of, we went again to the Sunrise restaurant, and had breakfast. He had a burger which (I tasted it) was horrible, but I guess he liked it. Then I brought up the idea that I had to go do email, and he told me that morning that he had to go to the tailor to have him make a dress for him. That he would have done at the Merkato, the biggest market in Africa (apparently). What we eventually decided to do is that we would take the taxi/bus to the Hilton, I would jump off there, while he would continue to the Merkato, and then he would meet me back there in a few hours, so to give me time to do my email.

And we did that. The Hilton was a relaxing experience. I was walking very slow, just like I had been doing for the past couple of days. I was still sick. I sat at a computer. They had a modem per machine, I believe. I downloaded my ssh, and did my email. 2 hours of email 300birr, which is about $36. A good 18 buck per hour. After that I was looking for my good friend, but couldn't find him, so I walked around and exchanged more money, and that was a very good idea. Then I tried to call the LVIA people, got a lady who told me that the Italians were out for lunch, to call them on the cell phone. I was not going to call the cell phone from the Hilton, as they were charging 10birr for that.

Afework finally arrived, and we walked around the hotel a little, since he had not seen it. Then we came straight back to the hotel. And I called the cell phone from the hotel and talked to the Italians lady,, whose name I do not know. I asked her for a doctor, and she told me the Hayat Hospital is where they always go. I wanted to rest, but they said they were running errands so I could meet them in 30 minutes at the Olympia building. So me and Afework hopped on a taxi and in taxi-mode we got there in 15 minutes. Waited there for a few minutes, and sure enough the Italians showed up. They were a little surprised to see Afework, but we took him with us to the hospital.

At the hospital, They took my blood pressure, heart rate, temperature, and 4ml of blood they tested for parasites, and counted WBC. then I had a urine test and a chest X-ray. At the end, it turned out I was exactly right. I thought that with the cold I got on the truck, I had gotten some bacterial infection in the lungs, which I called bronchitis. I thought I had to take antibiotics, but I was happy that I came to the hospital just to make sure I didn't have some tropical disease like Malaria. It turns out that bronchitis is more of a viral thing in the breathing passages, and that I had Pneumonia, which is a bacterial infection in my left lung. The doctor backed this up by saying that The blood samples were clean from all parasites, but there was a very high WBC count, 15000, instead of 4000-10000 (when he said this, I thought "dow, that's why I have fever!"). Then he pointed out that 18% of them were neutrophils, which indicates a bacteriological infection somewhere. From the urine sample he excluded kidney/bladder infection, and from the lung x-ray he could see a white spot, rather large in the left lung, which looked like some infection of some sort.

Then he gave me the option of whether to stay in the hospital for 2 days, so he could inject antibiotics IV, or to stay home and do it home. I decided the second option. I agreed that I would come back in a week to take another chest x-ray and pick up all my files/records, and I left with Afework to the LVIA headquarters. There Gabriele and the lady entertained us for very little time while wrapping things up, then we happen on the pickup truck in 4 in the front cabin and drove back toward town to look for a pharmacy. We found one, and I bought the medicines, then we came home. I said bye to them ans thanked them a lot, since they took me to the best hospital in town. And indeed I must say, it was a petty good hospital. They took so little blood from me, I didn't eve notice anything.

Afework stayed with me a little longer, until it was too late for him to catch a taxi in bus-mode home, so I gave him some money, 20birr for taxi-mode. I took my antibiotics and went to bed soon after he left.

I shortly thereafter woke up feeling very feverish. I put the thermometer on my head and I couldn't see anything. But I looked at it as I pulled it off, and I saw the colors go down from 40 down to 35. So I probably was VERY hot. So I popped 2 Paracetamol pills, as the doctor prescribed me to take in case I get fever. I took a long while, but eventually it went down again. And I was able to fall asleep again.

Woha what a day. Now I have to stay in bed for at least 3 days, as the doctor said. But he said to stay until all symptoms are gone. That is no cough or runny nose. I don't necessarily have to stay in bed until i finished the whole antibiotics cycle, but until all symptoms go away. That is the important part. I Hope it wont be too long. I hope to be better soon.
