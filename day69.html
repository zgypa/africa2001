Day 69 - Shahandi, Ethiopia

Wednesday August 1st, 2001

Bus to Metema was a trip. Incredible. It was pretty bad and the road got muddy and the bus kept on getting stuck. And the driver kept on holding tight on the steering wheel when we was getting pulled out of holes, so we eventually ran into some serious steering problems. We ended up having to bend the steering rod back by lifting the bus on jacks, then putting rocks under the bend of the rod, then dropping the weight of the bus onto it. And I was looking, and of course I don't understand a word of what they say, so I just looked, and for once I was able to understand what the hell they were doing, and said to myself: "Hmmm, yeah, this time it actually makes sense. Yeah, that's a pretty good idea." And in fact it worked pretty well. But then this truck, a caterpillar, was toeing us out of a very steep hole, and by the time we were out of the whole, the right front hub was perpendicular to the street, while the left one was parallel to it. We got saved by this army truck a russian 6wheeler which gave most of us a ride to the next town. That was an amazing trip. Finally on a kick-ass truck. This thing was really great, it could fly on any road, with 6 wheel drive and extra compressors for tires, so to have un-flattable tires. Great for driving through open fires. Sounds like fun. Mengistab a soldier I met on the truck promised to take me to Metema tomorrow. We do have an exhaust manifold problem, nonetheless.
