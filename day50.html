Day 50 - Hyke, Ethiopia

Friday July 13th, 2001

Today is departure day. We wake up at 5:30 so to be at the train station by around 6am, and we look for a bus to Weldyia. And we find one. The price was 48 birr each.

The bus leaves at around 8am. The way we enter the bus is by circling around it once. Not to mention that the circling around totally screwed up our positioning plan to get good seats. So we end up with basically no seats at all. Well we find them, but not even window seats, which are good for sleeping.

Dani kept on complaining for this girl in front who was very cute and little, but would puke all the time, and take craps... a toddler. And would smell like a warthog. But nobody would open the window..

The trip was not too exciting. We stopped at this little town for food, and we saw, actually, something that was pretty interesting. A kid who was begging. Right, you see those all over the place. But behind him there was another kid who was tapping on his shoulder and was telling him not to beg. Doing the "nono" finger and head shake. Dan noticed that at first.

The sceneries were great. But I was complaining because we couldn't see too much of them since we didn't have window seats, and since everybody had the curtains pulled to reduce sunlight.

At sunset, the bus stopped in a town which was not Weldyia, our final destination. We thought we would make it in one day to Weldyia, but in fact we did not. We stopped at Hyke, and stayed at the M. Hotel, just opened and unfinished about 1.5 months ago. It was an OK hotel, and we had food there, it was not bad. Before the food we had some juices at a bar, with a local 8th grader. Great juices, those Ethiopians juices are. And the 8th grader was pretty cool too. He was pretty eager with learning English.

Dani asked information about the new hotel we were staying at so he could send it to Phillip Briggs, his guide book.
