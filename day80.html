Day 80 - Khartoum, Sudan

Sunday August 12th, 2001

I decided to fly to Cairo. I will check for the flight prices, but I don't think they will be more than 250USD. And if they are I will try to go to the airport and see if I can standby.

Travelling by public transport is definetely not the way to travel through Africa, as you cannot get the best out of it. It is a good idea to see what it's like. And it's good to do if you work here, and have to just move from one city to the other. But havvig to cvarry your whole house with you makes it harder. You also don't get a good chance at seeing things,as sometimes you don't get window seats, or you are in the back of a truck, and cvan't see outside. You cannot stop and look at sceneries when you want. You can't buy all the souvenirs you want, because you know you can't carry them. The bottom line is that it's very difficult.

The trip from here to Cairo would be relaitvely easy apart the weather. I would be taking a train from here to Wadi Halfa, then from Halfa to Aswan Egypt there is a ferry. Once in Egypt there are all forms of transportatio available. But it would take at least 5 days just to get to Aswan. and another 1 or 2 days from there to Cairo.

I don't know, it's just that this flu I just got over kind of demoralized me a little: I feel physically a lot more vulnerable, since I got sick twice on this jouorney already. Time is running up. I think I should just fly. Plus if I fly, I can get to see the Meroe Pyramids, which I have already paid for. Becasue today is Sunday, and yesterday I didn't go buy the tickets becase I was not feeling well.

The schedule was: Saturday buy train and ferry tickets. Sunday go with bus to Meroe pyramids. Sleep there out on the desert by the pyramids. Monday morning hitch a ride down to Kabushyia, and hop on the train there. But with this flu, the whole schedule si screwed up, which means that if I want to go to Cairo via surface, I have to give up Meroe. Or, wait another week and be in Cairo in 2 weeks, which is not an option.
