Day 62 - Addis Ababa, Ethiopia

Wednesday July 25th, 2001

Wake up, meet neighbours, peace corp neighbours, from Kenya. Have breakfast next door. Good mini-pizza's and good donuts.

Stopped by Immigration for the visa extension. 105birr. Gotta pick it up tomorrow. Crazy building the immigration office.

I am in the need of getting out of Addis. I need to procede my journey. If I can't get a visa for Sudan, fuck it, I'll have to skip Sudan and fliy over it. So I look around for flights, but there are many that are too damn expensive. What I found from Yemenia was great, but not available. They put me on the waiting list for this weekends flight. I don't know what to do, I don't think this will come through, but if it does it would be fuckin' awesome Check this out: fly to Egypt through Sahan'a (Yemen). But the Sahna'a flight arrives 3 days before the next flight out to Egpyt. (This is what I found out I could do by going directly to their offices. They did not tell me this at the agency, so I was missing a lot of opportunities for getting out of there.) So I get to spend 3 days in Sahna'a free (no need for visa, whish are hard to get for there, for same islamic reasons that they are hard to get for Sudan.) just with the flight ticket as visa. Let's hope for the best. I like this deal a lot. They only need to call me tomorrow at the hotel telling me something opened. Yeah right, "ONLY".

So great. I come home at the Wutma Hotel to find out that the phone lines are still kaputt. Perfect. So this makes it a certainty that I will not recieve my phone call tomorrow. Also the Baro Hotel has problems with them. Which means no convenient internet for me. So I go try out some alternatives. Internet at CPU computer center. Many ports firewalled out and I am not able to do ssh. Discordia is not responding and it's .75birr/minute. So no email, only logbook.

So I tell my self: "Fuck all this Shit!" and I went to Pizzeria Don Vito. A very expensive (comparitavely) Italian pizzeria. And I must say it was indeed a good Italian pizzeria, and a pretty high class one too! Definetely more Italian pizzas there then in America. The situation is wierd, though. An Italian restaurant being served by Ethiopain ladies.

With my new Peace Corp friends we went in search for a nonexisting disco where students hang. Another failure. We ended up at the Memo Club. A place of hores. Off of Bole road, right after the big comunist star over the road. Extremley expensive too: 15birr to get in, 10 birr for bear, 8birr for water or warm soda. Cold soda yellem. Fuckin'... And the toilets: toilet paper: only by demanding the Housekeeper will she give you a piece or two of "soft" (toiletpaper).

Bah. So I decide to check out the back, right next to the toiles by the housekeeper, a young girl with a name that I forget already. There I meet all sorts of girls, one of which (Tsahi?) takes my brown hat and goes for a ride. Then another comes, so I ask why the hell don't they simply put the toilet paper in the toilets, since the place is so damn expensive? But the girls gave me a very straight answer, one that I was not looking for, but that I guess I had to take since it qulified for an answer-allright. It was "But you see, if somebody needs more paper, they can just ask for more at the beginning, and if somebody doesn't use it all, they can always take it back!". It felt kind of hopeless to try to explain that that was exactly what I was trying to avoid in the first place. Basically I asked them why do they try to save toilet paper, and they answered that it wasn't that bad to save toilet paper. Does it actually mean "saving is part of our culture, and also if we are rich, we still try to save", or does it mean "we need to employ people, and this way we get a job for a housekeeper"?

When I went back inside, a girl finally approaches me: she seems nice and turns out to be japo-ethiopian. She wanted to come back home with me, in bed, that is. Why didn't I take her home? But then again: There does not have to be a why. I mean, I always do something for a reason: I stop and ponder, then I always have a reson for what I am doing. But in this case, she was very close to me. It was done already. All I had to do is to take her hand and leave. And I really wanted to. She was intersting, and I kinda liked that japanese in her. But instead I was strong and decided not to. But what really made me not want to do it is the speculation that I might not enjoy it in a night like this one. And this actually could be true. I mean, I would only have 4 hours until it's 7.30 in the morning. But, OK, say I would wake up at 10 to go do stuff. Yes that's true, I could have done it: spend 6 hours with her, maybe start talking naked and smoking up a little,... But then again, I want to do it when I have nothing to do the next morning. So here I go. I at least could have had a good fuck after such a day, but I blew that one off too.

Guess what? I forgot my knife there, my camping knife.
