Day 81 - Cairo, Egypt

Monday August 13th, 2001

• Then at 1am or so, I decide to become part of the mayham of securty and work my way in the interior of the airport, where they have aircon. At the cafetereia (upstaris, no aircon) I meet Ruggero, from Napels, who is into Construction and was working on building the Hilton of Port Sudan. He started talking and didn't stop. He told me about his wife, who had ictus, and became paralized just 2 years ago, and this trip was like a breake from the tedious life he was leading trying to make her feel as if she still had a life. He always said he would go to Egypt with her, but now that she's paralyzed, he realizes it will not happen, so he simply decided, out of respect, not to go to Egpypt at all. His daughter is in biomed tech sales of some sort. He also told me how he spend the first 40 years of his life in Italy, then moved to Nigeria with family and all. Then came back, dropped off one daughter in some private school in Rome with a brother in law, but then he moved to Sicily, with the daughter. So then he moved to Sicly to for a while.... Oh, he just went on talking. Now he was heading back home after the headaches of trying to get stuff done in Africa, which we all know a little about. Me just because I lived in Italy for a while. He also told me about his cure for his tyoroid problem. He got an injection of alcohol and something else I forgot directly into the gland, and it shrunk like 90% (which is very good, apparently). He also thinks that people here are more dumb than in Europe making the example that all the technology and stuff installed here, they don't know how to work it, also after weeks and weeks of training. He divirged the topic of conversation, maybe realizing that this was not a good ground to make such a statement, by saying that the colonialism ended too abruptly. And there he might have a point. Maybe if the brits, and french, and italians and so on would have stayed longer it would have helped the africans get used to things a little. On the other hand it would have created other problems, like in South Africa with Apartheid and such... who knows?

• On the plane we lost contact, and I made contact with another man, who was offering me to help me out in cairo, which was a great relief. But then, after he picked up his luggage, he said he would wait for me outside, but still had to change money (which he apparently forgot about). After I changed it, of course was not there anymore. I actually found him in the bus going downtown (which I had to find myself). Sometimes people who seem helpful they might be, but they are so unthinking (aren't they everywhere, though?) that they become unhelpful again. Like this guy. Also if he forgot I was changing money, and was really waiting outside, and didn't see me come: where did he think I was disappearing? Like that I flew back to Khartoum, or something?

• The time machine over here was incredible. It felt strange. I can really feel the effects of it. I mean how much of a time machine an airplane actually is. Now that I have felt what it is like to do miles the hard way, being in Khartoum with no ticket or way to Cairo on sunday morning, and checking in at the Sun hotel in Cairo at 10am of Monday morning really makes you ponder. it should have taken me at least 6 days to do the trip....

• The hotel is nice. I am staying in a drom room with 4 other guys, one australiand, a guy from danmark, who looks very good. Definetely very cute. I can see many girls liking his features. His name is Casper.

• He showed me around a little, since he is leaving tomorrow and has seen a little more of Egypt than what I have seen. He took me to this marvellous Internet Café, the best one I have seen in my life. It's a high class café with, in a corner, 5 computers or so, with a fast connection (maybe cable modem? I didn't ask, but I was downloading from brillig at 12KB/s), and, of course, shisha, which you can smoke at the computer, which I have of course done. The rpices are not too high, compared to western prices. Maybe they are high for here, but it's worth it. That place is really "Cool".

• Talked to Guy, he called me at the hotel, after getting my email. I informed myself looking at Casper's Egypt lonely planet guide, and it seems it only takes 7 hours to Teba, which is at the border with Israel, then I can take a taxi or something to Elat, but I think Guy can pick me up there. It all depends how soon he can be there. 'Cuz I could probably be there Thursday.

• I lucked out, since Casper seems to be a nice guy. He is going tomorrow to Giza, and to the Museum, which is exactly what I wanted to do. So we will wake up at 7am, and see if we can get there.
