Day 90 - Jerusalem, Israel

Wednesday August 22nd, 2001

• Today Guy took me to see Yavd Vashem, this museum built on Mt. Herzl (Theodor Herzl was the one who wrote "Der Judenstaat") in memory of the Holocoaust. It's a big campus with mnuments and expositions all over, including a childrens memorial, a room full of candles, or so it looks like. There are mirrors, and aas you enter all you see are small candlelights all over, and you hear a voice reading out the name and the age of the children that perished in the holocaust. Then there is a sort of sinagogue, which is used for ceremonies, with a sort of fireplace in the middle, and an art museum, with artwork that was taken away by the Nazis. Also a children's musem with all children's article and memories and videos with stories. Then there is a train cart, a german train fart on a bridge which is only half buillt, to symbolize the deportation. It probably is an origianl cart, knowing how the Israeli's don't fuck around.. At the end they built a sort of labyrinth, more or less in the shape of Europe. It's more like many rooms with no roof, than a labyrynth, and in every room on the wall there are names of towns and cities which had Jewish comunities. Definetely a job well done. Lots of money put into it, but it was nothing new for me. Definetely not as interesting as the old city tour, but pretty impressive.

• The same nite we went to Tel Aviv, where I met Smira, Guy's mum, she gave me an Esprit t-shirt and told me she was going to go to study English, and the next tie I would come she would speak fluent English. Guy said "roouuaa--t". They showed me Guy's dad pictures, some of them at least, they showed me Guy's sister's bedroom, and memories. Smira is very nice and fun and good looking. She seems to like me too. And me? I like her, sure. There a little of comunicaiton barrier, but she seems happy and cool and fun.

• guy's house, where he grew up is pretty small. Reminds me of Mauro's house, I don't know why, although it's alot smaller than his old house.

We ate in this expensive place, and Gal was telling me how they might not let me in, because of my clothes. I hadn't even shaved and had dirty pants, and a shitty t-shirt... Tomer plays percussion and told us about this Terry Bozio video he saw and how he is totally sweet and gay during the interview, but then when he plays he undergoes this metamorphoses. With Yossi I had little to do, and didn't talk much.

• The Gay club was basically a club scene: Gal gave me one of his black shirts (btw, I've gotta buy one of those) and we went in, and he bought me a malibu-orange, and got himself a beer. Loud 80's music (it seems very popular in Israel here now). Then we went out to meet Guy, and I decided to go home with guy and corinna (she had to leave by 1am, since she ahd to work the next day so I was kind of making plans to come back to Tel Aviv on my own with a bus or with Tomer, who is recording in Jersualem, and was going to do the trip in the morning) to go home with them then. We said by to Gal, I might meet him in Italy, when he comes. I hope so, he is a nice guy. And fun. Everytime i hang out with him I have this wierd feeling of comfortableness...

The tow nites before I was up and could here noisesof annoyance from the bed until I would turn the light off, and do my things in the dark, then peace would reign. All good.
