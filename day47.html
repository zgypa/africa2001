Day 47 - Addis Ababa, Ethiopia

Tuesday July 10th, 2001

So today is tuesday. And I wake up, call the embassy, they tell me to all alter, so I go change some money. I dress up heavily, and off I go. i change all the travelers cheques I have left, which is $60. Then I come back and run into the Berkeley dudes. They say that they were going to check out the bus station to buy tickets for tomorrow to go to Harar, and I asked if I could join, since I wanted to know where it was so I could then go to buy tickets, maybe in a few days, for Gondar.

Before we leave, I try to call the Sudan embassy again, but they tell me that the VISA dude is not in, so to cal tomorrow.

So we go, and the dude with the beard, David, after some walking around the Merkato area, gets stressed out, and says that he can't take it anymore (since we could not find the bus station) and took off towards the hotel. He was also starting to feel sick. So me and the other dude proceeded to discover we made a wrong left turn. Eventually we found the bus stop, and discovered that my trip to Gondar would be 2 days (on night stopover only) and 77birr. While his trip to Harar would be some 13h and around 53birr. This was a surprise for him, since he thought it would be around the 30birr. So he didn't buy the tickets and we proceeded to look for a taxi in bus-mode to go home. And indeed we did, and were also able to say no to a taxi in taxi-mode and went off to find a minibus that took us straight to piazza, and we then walked home paying 6-7 times less. He thanked me for the interesting experience, although he had already taken those kind of public transportation. I also made him save a bir or so, as he was asking for the price inside the minibus. I told him not to do that, but just to look at what the others paid. I also told him never to pay more than 1.40birr, since that was the price of the longest trip one coudl possibley take around town.

When we came back, I decided to call it a day, and wanted to rest. I didn't want to stretch it and risk a fever.

But after dinner, on my way to my room, I run into another Daniel, this time from New York. We talk up until past midinte. He tells me about the Kite constellation that I found, which, in fact, is called the Southern cross. He tells me about the Solar Eclipse, and all his travels and writing adventures. I tell him about my ex job in Neurosurgery. We agree to meet tomorrow to go run some errands together.
