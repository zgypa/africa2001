Day 54 - Laliblea, Ehiopia

Tuesday July 17th, 2001

Today was the extra chill day. The day that was not supposed to exist. I mean we added, we decided to stay an extra day, since the day of yesterday was so good. So we decided to go to the Monastery, the Asheten Mariam Monastery at the top of a hill, like at 3700m. A 2 hour hike. Very pleasant. Dan was very fast in walking up. I had to slow down. We had two kids as guides, one of them was the boy with the red shirt, which is the guide that Dan wanted to begin with.

And with them we ascended the mountain. On the way up we ran into some farmers, highland farmers, and Dan started to beat the barley with them. It was great. He was also really good at it! I wish I had a picture of it. Then all the kids, as you ascend, the farmer kids start to follow you. They can go up and down 10 times a day before they get tired. No problem. They are used to doing it with 50kg bags of barley on their back.

Up there, the monastery was great: There was a knife of land behind the monastery that extended a little into the void of the valley. On the shoulder of the blade of the knife, just on the edge, there lay a shithole, a man made concrete shithole. I took advantage of the location of the shithole, and took a crap there, at 3700m looking down at Lalibela and its valley over a 30m drop. And Dan took advantage of my shitting and took pictures of the whole event. Pictures with details...

Another amazing scene up there was the cloud scene: They were amazing. As the storm was moving in and out (the rain season) you could see these clouds going up and down the mountains, now making it very foggy, now clearing it up. You could just stand on that blade and be amazed.

Here is what I wrote as Dan was taking a picture of me in the rain, on the top by the monastery, typing on the visor: "ZMonapstery of . 3700m. A rock sticking out with 220 degrees of view over aawwnafenwifia a mthe w iner,,mceani i am lookig at the hemisphere of the world. aI amout hthere. damn!!!!! hrlp i am flying......and i am writing all this odwn . all i am sayiojgi am writing down."

And indeed that was a crazy experience, as we had smoked up quite a bit, so it really felt like I was flying in the valley! Wow. It was really great. I could have stayed there for days. I can understand how the priests live. With lots of grass!

And the descent was a lot easier then going up. Dan, decided to buy one of the sheppard's hats for his dad so he did from a guy that was walking down with us. But he bought one that was too small (he only realized this later, you know, when you buy something, it takes you a while to find out whether you really like it or not). So he found this out only once he ran into a bigger one on the way down. The diagnosis of the situation was simple: Dan had made a mistake. According to his life filosofy, there was no problem with this, as long as he fixed it. So he tried to see if he could get his money back from that one guy. Then buy the hat from the other hat-seller-girl. But the guy, obviously, didn't want to exchange again. So Dan offered him the hat back (cost 25birr) in exchange of only 15 birr. The boy would then be left with the hat and 10birr, sounds like a deal for him, right? But the boy preferred 25birr today, than 35birr tomorrow, and didn't want to do the trade.

Eventually Dan succeeded by giving the girl the hat he had bought and 10birr in exchange of the bigger hat. Now, that was not too easy neither. It was hard to convince the girl that that was actually a deal for her. But she finally agreed. And then, at the same time, also the boy agreed, that is he uderstood that he would be making a deal out of the exchange he had, upto a few minutes ago, not wanted to do. But Dan was firm and told him that he realized to late, and lost his opportunity.

So Dan got his hat, and everyone was happy. I must say that our little boy-guides did a great job in translating. Without them the whole deal would have been impossible.

Lots of beggers, but when we got down, we took our buddies to the Asheton where we paid them. It turnes out we met Werner there. He was a guy I had met in Addis, when he had just came back up from his 9 day Omo Valley safari trip.

And Dan started having lots of fun with this kid as he found out that Werner was a profesional Skuba diver. He was doing cave mapping in different underwater caves in the red sea and Australia. Gosh what some interesing adventurous stories:120m deep dives, kicking the silt, masks smashing onto your face, thining problems... Wow. Dan was really entertained. Or rather, let's just say that Werner gained many credibility/reliability points for Dan.

Now we wanted to have some food. So we went to look in the main square, but couldn't find anything. Finally we found a place, but they were going to take too long to prepare the food, so we just decided to go to our hotel and to eat there instead. And that's what we ended up doing. I took a shower that night too. A shower with warm water heated up in big tin cans over fire...
