Day 64 - Addis Ababa, Ethiopia

Friday July 27th, 2001

A pretty straight forward day. Me and Dan. We go to the Sudan Embassy drop off passport. And then to the Memo Night Club to pick up forgotten knife.

Normal looking teens that were inviting me to do something. The action of the hands indicated carrying something, bus since I was carrying nothing, I excluded the possibility of them offering to carry something for me.

Then I took the wrong minibus in bus-mode, so got off and started asking for the minibus that headed towards Piazza. The directions I was getting were very misleading,and out of the 3 people interrogated, I couldn't get anything useful: they all indicated a different direcction. Only then I realized that I actually knew exactly where we were, sice I got off as soon as I relized that the minibus was going the wrong way. And I simply headed back, to were the minibusses to Piazza do drive to pick one up.

On the way there is when I happened to hit the Eyo-Ta Supermarket, located behind the Stadium, which happened to coincidentally be the plae where I realized we were going the wrong way, just before I got off that minibus. The girls in there (the storekeepers and helpers and family) are crazy, but crazy in a good way. They like to play a lot. Like this girl was insisting to marry me, and was offering me a ring, which coincidentally was on her left ringfinger, but was supposed to have no marrital meaning whatsoever.

Dan to a beggar: "I appreciate all you have done for me, but I still wont give you anything",obviously referring to one of the just coined beggee rules: "I only give money to people who tender a service, seniors and cripples. 'Cuz what else are they gonna do?"

15:37 "Christ in a bag" (danglass66)

"Fruit House" by Giorgis Sq. is supposeed to have the best juices in town.

Spend 45 minutes in an italian bookstore called "Libreria Italiana" looking for sometihng affordable to buy. Luciano DeCrescenzo was between 120-130EB. Eventually I decided on Italo Calivino, Il barone rampante which was only 28EB. These were the only people I met that could speak italian (apart from the Italians I met). And they just learned it from other Ialians, or in the Italian school that is there.

Head back to Wutma and meet Dan, and we have lunch there (lasagna, cold but good). Then we hung the map of ethiopia on the wall in his room, and he started to plan his trip. This planning, I would say, took a good 1.5h, thats 90 minutes of staring at a wall with a map on it. One thing I can say about him: he is definetely very thorough when making decisions.

So I took off in the searh of some recording unit similar to his. I thought it would in fact be good idea to record some noises. I picked up Shereef, and he took me to Merkato. There I kinda lost hope for it, since the only stores that had portable recorders, these were shitty, and din't have a MIC IN jack. But I got to see the Merkato, the famous Merkato, which is supposed to be the largest in Africa. Walking down these alleys I realized what would be the most touristy, or rather, authentic tourist/souvenir shirt: TITANIC. But instead I bought a FILA rain jacket, no hood, double-face, grey and blue for 140EB.

Eventually we got to this Supermaket, apparently the biggest in Addis, but I 'm not sure, were I find a big fat nothing at first. Not even the AUTAN bug repellant (which I had seen in another supermeket weeks ago, but was too expensive) was an item they could provide me with and still gain a lot of pleaseure from opening and closing doors.
