Day 83 - Cairo, Egypt

Wednesday August 15, 2001

• $1=4.51E£

• Sudan Embassy to change dinar. Indicated me the Abu Sinbl Hotel in Attaba square. Hard to find. Changed 8750SD for 100E£ losing ca.$10. Better than nothing.

• Khan Khali market to buy shisha. Hard: 132E£ for:

  • 2 good pipes
  • 1 green glass
  • 1 heavy duty tube
  • 1 ebony mouth piece
  • 3 burners
  • 1 brass tweezers
  • 2 heavy duty chromed dishes
• 5E£ for 250g tobacco pouches.

• Trasformi l'idea di avare un figlio in una prigione. Bel pensioro e modo di esprimiere quelli che, come cinzia, a volte, si taglia le gambe quando ha un figlio, perche' fare cose con lui/lei sarebbe un problema inveced id affrontare le difficolta' e continuare a fare tutto cio' che si faceva prima entro limiti) anche con il fitlio (un po' alla Arianna).

• Here at the Sun hotel one can here Cairo. As i said , it's a very big city, and honking is a very big activity. From this crossing, one of the most important ones in the city, Tahrir square, one can hear as every single driver that passes by drops it's share of acoustic pollution by honking at least for millisecond: Bp! while others just drive around, and you (engineer) ask yourself if they actually wired the honk switch closed. I have also seen a bicycle with a bell connected to the front wheel, and everytime the bicycle is moving brrrrrrRRRRRRrrrrrrrrRRRRRRRRRRrrrrrrrrrrRRRRRRRRRrrrrrrrrrrrrRRRRRRRRRrrrrrrrrrrrRRRRRR and the "ing" never comes. It's just crazy.

• I keep spitting white flem, this is getting boring. I wish I could produce some energy from i, burn it or donate it. I feel like I want it to be useful, if it has to continue for a longer period of time.
