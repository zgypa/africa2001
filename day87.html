Day 87 - Jerusalem, Israel

Sunday August 19th, 2001

• Todfay was fix it day. What we did was basicallly get the car open and be able to drive off. It entailed going to the airport to pick up the package with the keys that Ruthi sent over air freight, then calling the insurance towing truck, who arrived around 3pm, and having him tow the car to the electrician of Eilat. The tow truck driver was very nice. Also the electrician. They did a good work, and bypassed the immobilizer with no major problem, then actually soldered the starter wires, where the bypass was. That, for me, is a good job. Then we went back to Corinna, who was still on the beach and picked her up, then picked up our stuff from the Hotel, and at about 4-5 we took off for Jerusalem, on the dangerous road. The dangerous road passes through some palestinian land, just before Jerusalem, but it's some 100 km shorter. We decided it tobe safe to drive through there, although there was a miscaclculation: We thought it was day, but by the time we got there it got to be nite. Oh well, but all went well.

• Guy's apartment here is like a little studio, one room, but very nice. we cooked some sauce with this technique that Corinna has to cook peppers, which is great. They turn out to be basically roasted. She puts them in olive oil, and lets them on low fire for like 30-40 minutes.

• A little of Campari, a little of internet, and then I went to bed. Corinna was complaining for the noise and the light in a very winy fashion. I was kind of annoyed by it, but on the other hand I could understand that she wanted to sleep.
