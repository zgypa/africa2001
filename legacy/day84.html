Day 84 - Eilat, Israel

Saturday August 16th, 2001

• Wake up in Cairo at around 6am, and I took a cab to the Tourgoman bus station to catch the 7.30 bus. The cab charged me 7.50Elbs.

• At bus station the bus was filled so I bought tickets for the 9.30 bus. I had to wait there for a few hours. That was most difficult. With the busses honking and getting really close to the chair I was sitting at, and with the stink of trash all over I was about to lose it. I was happy I was going to leave and see guy soon.

• Met Rob on the bus. He was also going to Taba, but with a bike (he put it on the bus and they charged him double, another 50Elbs for it.

• He showed me some pictures (slides) he did, while we were o the bus. Some good stuff. He told me about his 14mm corrected lens (corrected, so it does not distort.) interesting. I want to check some of those out. He showed me some tricks of how to use flash with long exposition, thus freezing humans, or moving objects, yet getting in the light of scenery and dead objects in. A great idea.

• The drive through the desert was very interesting. We stopped at Nakhl, a small town, like an oasis, were it is really hot, but dry. You don't really feel the heat too much, and the walls of the houses (white)) are not too hot neither. But the place was deserted, still a reminance of the Israelis.

• I met this guy there, who was asking me if I wanted to smoke some Bango (which is grass) but i DIDN'T have time really, and I didn't want him to give me any, becasue I didnn't trust him, and also because I was going to cross the Israeli border in a few hours.

• More desert sceneries were amazing. From sand dunes to dirt desrt, and eventually to deserted mountains, or hills. We emerged out of the hills, and I could see the blueness of the Red Sea stick out from the bright brown of the deserted hills... It was all just amazing.

• Crossing the border was a procedure in itself, even more for Rob, who had to constatnly take his bike apart, and put it back together. He has a trailer to. First the security check at the Egyptian side, then the passport stamp at the egyptian side. Then you enter the Immigration building on the Israaeli side, which looks like an airport. Just amazing. The Egyptian side, it seems like they got a little intimidated by it, so they built their side similar. It also looks like an airport, but not as much as the Israeli side. Then you do the Israeli security check, were a guy litterally checked and touched every corner of my body, apart the crotch. He did that part with a mettal detector. Then the israeli passport stamnp, then the Isralei customs (they didn't even check there, they just stamped my paper). Also, at the security check, they give you a small paper, with 4 stages: Security, Passport, Customs, and Car/Vehicle check. At aevery stage you get stanmped. Then, when you exit the gate-airport-border office/building, the guardian asks you for that piece of paper, and if you don't have it, hw wont let you out.

• There is a bus every half hour that connects the Taba border with Eilat and costs 5.70NIS. The exchange rate is anyware between 3.80-4.15NIS depending if you change at a hotel or bank.

• I told Rob that I would meet him at the information center in Eilat. So he biked, and I waited for the bus. I saw him from the bus as he was pedalling with all the weight of his trailer and smiled... I wanted to shout to him, but I couldn't as the windows were closed inside the coach.

• The information center was closed, but not just closed for the day, but closed for good. There were a few telephone numbers at the door, but the lady at the desk said she would only take care of the Jazz Festival, that is going to happen here at the end of the month (27-29August). Martin Medeski and Wood are playing here, as well as Take 6 and ron carter, billy cobham in an interesting band.

• Rob came, and we went for the Red See Hotel and found out that their prices were very high :30NIS for the roof and no bathroom or shower, 80NIS for dorm room, and 100NIS each for a 6 bedroom room that we could have used in 4. So Rob decided to discuss in front of a beer, so we had 2 pints each. The beer sunk into me and I got quikly drunk. We eventually decided, after talking to a couple, which gave us some good advies, then liquidated us, i.e. got rid of us, by saying they had very important things to talk about, to go up to downtown and look there.

• So we did and fount the Taba Hostel, for 35NIS/nite, and checked in there. Locked up everything, then headed for the bus statino, were I called Guy, Rob called Yuval ( af riend in Tel Aviv), went to another pub, got a huge salad, then met Guy at the Texas Grill, then moved to another bar on the sea were we had some more drinks and hummus...

• Then we went back to the hostelI picked up my stuff, said by to Rob, who was going to leave fro Tel Aviv the next morning and headed over to our hotel, which was a lot better.
