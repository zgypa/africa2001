Day 78 - Khartoum, Sudan

Friday August 10th, 2001

• The day today was a complete rest. I am not feeling to well, so I stayed at the sailing club with a headache trying to get some rest.

• The Swiss guys said that the VISA extension they are trying to get would take a few days, util saturday, which measn we cannot go to Meroe together neither today, nor tomorrow. I think I will have to go alone following my initial plan.

• At around 3pm the air started to get thicker, as the wind kept blowing. The atmospere was very reddish, the wind was probably blowing up some dus from the desert around. AT some pioont the air was so brown, that even the Nile looked blue. And that was when my tempetation of jumping in was the greatest.

• Cultural exchange with the Swiss (Philippe and Marcel from Losanne): I gave them the Calvino book, they gave me Andrea DeCarlo, Di noi tre. Marcele almost finished my book. He is liking it. I am happy he is. I liked it too. Maybe we can talk about it once he is done.
