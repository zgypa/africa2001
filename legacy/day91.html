• Today we went out to the Dead Sea, had a bathe in the water. Amazing. You just float, just like you see on TV or in the pictures. As soon as you are standing in one foot deep water you can feel it already. It's like the water is pushig you up more, you are lighter. Then, when you are in, if you don't pay attention, you turn around very easily, especially if you are not straight with your back. And if you get the salt in your eyes, it's trouble. The burn just wont go away, like it does in sea water. It just stays. Yougotta go out and rinse in the boiling hot showers. I mean, you have to realize that this beach is in the middle of the desert, so it's kind of like an oases. And have you ever tried to taste it? Have you? Well, it's very salty. Like, extremely salty. Like, you coulnd't even boil pasta in it, because it's too salty. They say it's 10 times saltier than sea water.

When you get out and take a shower, and then dry off, your skin feels very soft, and you are clean. All that dirt that had accumulated under your nails is now gone. And all the bruses, well they burnn quite a bit when you are in the water, but then they are clean again. And your hair too. All clean. It's weird. But very cool.

Then we had dinner in this restaurant, which seemed to have a good deal for the meal, like 44 shekels for a soup, a main course, and a coffee. Which was OK, cuz if you bought them all seperately, it would have cost you like maybe 70 shekels. But when I asked him about the 44nis menu, he said, of course, that it was only for lunch.

Then there was this girl, wiatress, sort of young, showing her belly with a tight top (everyone wares tight tops here, this year. Even the ugly girls. Only if they have really small breasts, do they wear a looser shirt), and everytime I would look at her to get her attention because I needed service, she would look at me as if I looked at her because I liked her. Then Guy pointed out that maybe she looks at me that way because she likes me. That was a good point I hadn't thought about.

The other waitress, instead, wearing tight pants and shirt, had a much more beautiful body. She was maybe in her mid teens, and was walking in such a way, that I could have hired her as a model for my clothes, had I been a fashion designer. She just knew it. She knew she was good, and she just did it all the way. When she walked past my table the first time, she left an aura of top model, that i couldn't do anything else then laugh. I thought that if all the hot girls are like that, then I should have no problem (chigr yellem) in picking one up.

Then we got the bill, which included service, but as the waiter said, it didn't include tip. So we, and the table next to use, concluded that he was trying to get more money from us, and concluded that service was tip.

So then we went to the mini supermarket and had a flash back of how it was to do shoppig with Guy in the good old Cleveland days. We got stuck, like as usual: I propose something, he is not covinced, so I let go, and let him decided. But he is not ready for that, he just expressed that he was not convinced with what I had decided. Then he suggests something, or asks for some quality of cookies to buy. I look at him, and I really don't care, so I tell him. He gets stuck again, and doesn't know what to buy. So we end up walkig up and down the supermarket for hours. This time, though, he was good at recongizeing how it would go (he was faster than me), so we decided to decide 50% each,in terms of items. So I decided the liquer, and he decided the munchies. Then we bought some candels too.

We asked the cashier guy where we could find some peace and look at the stars. He told us to go behind the hilton, but we both agreed that would not be a good place, so Guy drove in the inland, away from the dead sea. Some 20-30km in, he pulls up in this drive way that goes 100m off the road, then we get our stuff, and start walking up a small hill. The moon was still up and it was going down. it was about 10pm. I really wanted to go in the desert and see the stars, but I wanted to go were the desert was flat, all flat, and no cities. The place were we went wasn't bad, I mean we had plenty of sky view, and the moon was setting. The only problem was that we had about 6-7 city/towns within a 30-40km radius, and we could see their lights leaking into the dark sky, washing out the stars in that corner. But for Israel, I think it was a good place. I should have done some starwatching in Egypt. Israel is too small.

And we stayed there, and open the wine bottle, and talked and drank, and discovered a way of lying on my full size therma-rest in two (which I had failed to find when with Corina some 2 years ago), and we talked about life, meditation, and what to do and what not. And we talked about what had been done, and I told him some Cleveland stories. Then, at around 1or 2am we decided to head back towards the car, because we were getting cold (it was very windy on that hill) and tired. So we work our way down, and soon realize that the moonlight would have been nice to have. We also realize how Guy's idea (suppressed by me) of buying another flashlight was a really good one. I forgot that you need one flashlight per person. And we went down, me first, because I am more confident in going down than I am in going up, and because I had boots, while he only had sandals. So I would go down a few feet, then I would stop and make light for him. And we went on like that for a while, and making decisions on where we came from. We were getting less and less secure about the path. We were starting to getting paranoid. We were about to freak out, and we knew it. But we didn't say a word about it. We knew that we could just walk to the road, and then we would find the car from there. And that's what we ended up doing at the end. On the way, I was thinking of how when you get lost, you always miss your target by jus a few feet. And then guy found the car. We had passed it by about 100m and missed it by maybe 5m. It was a relieve. We werent' tired anymore, but were suddenly aware of all the sweat that we had accumulated. We drove back to Jerusalem, with Guy trying to fight his tiredness by talking constantly.
